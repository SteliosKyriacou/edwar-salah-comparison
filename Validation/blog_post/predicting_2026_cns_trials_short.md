# Predicting the Outcome of 2026 CNS Clinical Trials

**March 2026**

CNS drug development has the worst success rate in pharma. Only ~7% of drugs entering Phase I in neuroscience ever reach approval. We ran 19 late-stage CNS candidates — spanning Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, ALS, Huntington's, and rare ataxias — through our multi-agent AI system to predict which ones will make it.

Here are the predictions, locked in before trial readouts.

## The Takeaway

**Tavapadon is the clear predicted winner** — 4x the CNS base rate and in a different league from the rest of the field.

The bottom of the table is dominated by **repurposed oncology drugs** (Nilotinib, Masitinib) — molecules designed for cancer that are being tried in dementia. The system flags these as having safety profiles incompatible with chronic neurodegenerative use.

The uncomfortable truth for the rest of the cohort: **most of these candidates have predicted success rates at or below what you'd expect by chance for a CNS drug**. The median prediction is 4.5%.

We'll revisit these predictions as trial results come in.
